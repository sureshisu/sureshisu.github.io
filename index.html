Biography

I'm an aerospace engineer with a focus on applied physics, experienced in computational magneto-hydrodynamics and fluid modeling in plasma physics. Currently, I'm a postdoctoral research associate at the Translational AI Center (TrAC), exploring machine learning and high performance computing using GPU programming for fluid dynamic and magneto-hydrodynamic simulations. I received my Ph.D in Aerospace Engineering from Virginia Tech, where my research focused on studying fusion-relevant liquid metal dynamics for Z-Pinch fusion experiments. My areas of interest includes magneto-hydrodynamics for engineering applications and space physics, fluid modeling in plasma physics, electromagnetism and magneto-hydrodynamic turbulence. I'm also interested in exploring relativistic magneto-hydrodynamics. Outside of work, I enjoy swimming, hiking, snowboarding, and reading fiction.
